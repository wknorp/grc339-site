Quarantine Hobbies

Dancing

Before shelter in place started, I signed up to dance in Cal Poly’s Spring Dance Show. Instead of canceling the show, we are now putting on a virtual version of the show. I’ve been learning choreography through videos and turning in footage of myself dancing in my backyard and garage. All of the dancers' footage will be compiled into edited videos that will be shown in a virtual production.

Yoga

The best part of quarantine has been yoga studios uploading virtual content for a much more affordable price. About every night I take a yoga or cardio class from Spark Yoga in SLO to keep my body and mind healthy in quarantine.

Playing with my Dogs

Sadly, we lost our very old dog Roxy during quarantine. Quarantine allowed me to get to spend a few final weeks with her and my other two dogs, a sweet Australian labradoodle named Quincy and a funny chihuahua named Oscar. As pictured above, the new goal is teaching Quincy to give me hugs.

Animal Crossing

A childhood favorite game of mine that just came out on Nintendo Switch has led me to spend hours building up my virtual island and paying off debts to a raccoon. Sounds weird, but I promise it’s fun.

Soaking up some Vitamin D

I spent most days for the first month of quarantine doing homework outside in the sun to avoid being too cooped up in quarantine. It was great, but now I’m boycotting the outdoors due to incessant allergy attacks :/

Baking

Banana Bread. So. Much. Banana Bread. With our bananas getting ripe faster than we can eat them in quarantine, it has been a non-stop bake session of my mom's incredible banana bread recipe.
